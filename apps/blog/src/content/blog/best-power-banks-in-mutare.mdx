## One Number to Understand First: mAh

Every power bank has a capacity listed in **mAh** — milliamp hours. Think of it like litres in a water tank. The bigger the number, the more charge it holds.

To make this practical, we'll use **4,000mAh as the standard phone battery** throughout this guide — which is roughly what most mid-range Android phones carry. If your phone has a 5,000mAh battery, the numbers will be slightly lower. If it's older and smaller, slightly higher.

With a 4,000mAh phone:
- A **10,000mAh** power bank charges it **~2 times**
- A **20,000mAh** power bank charges it **~4 times**
- A **27,000mAh** power bank charges it **~5–6 times**
- A **30,000mAh** power bank charges it **~6–7 times**

The reason it's not exactly 2× or 4× is that some energy is always lost in the conversion process — heat, cable resistance, and so on. A realistic efficiency rate is around 80–85%, which is what the estimates above account for.
